Delta Stool

A journey in geometric precision and craftsmanship

The Genesis

From Concept to Form

The Delta Stool began as an exploration of geometric minimalism. The challenge was clear: create a piece that would be visually striking from every angle while maintaining perfect stability and comfort.

Height 18 inches (optimal seating height)
Material Laminated hardwood
Design Tri-legged triangular geometry

Ideation

Exploring different geometric forms and materials through sketches, focusing on the intersection of stability and visual lightness. Each iteration refined the triangular form while maintaining structural integrity.

Initial Prototype

First iteration using lumber to test structural integrity and ergonomics. This phase was crucial in understanding the interplay between material strength and design aesthetics.

Material Selection

Careful selection of hardwood for lamination, considering grain direction and strength. Each component was chosen to enhance both the structural performance and visual appeal of the final piece.

Geometric Refinement

Fine-tuning the triangular form for perfect balance and visual harmony. Advanced calculations ensured that every angle and proportion contributed to both stability and aesthetic appeal.

Precision-engineered joints ensure structural integrity while maintaining clean lines
Carefully calculated angles create an illusion of floating geometry
The Result

Form Meets Function

The final design achieves a perfect balance between aesthetic beauty and functional comfort. The triangular faces create dynamic visual interest from every angle, while the 18-inch height provides optimal ergonomics for everyday use.
