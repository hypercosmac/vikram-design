Featured Project

Joule Thief Lamp

An accessible lamp powered by residual energy from discarded batteries, designed to provide light for those without reliable electricity access.

April 2019 - November 2019
Group Project - Team Lead

The Challenge

During a visit to Cottolengo Special School, I witnessed students struggling with frequent power cuts. Children had to have dinner in the dark and were scared to use the restroom at night. This experience revealed a broader challenge: 1.2 billion people worldwide lack access to reliable electricity.

Impact Areas


  • Students unable to study after dark

  • Safety concerns in dormitories and pathways

  • Difficulty during essential activities like dining

The Solution

After extensive research and 12 failed attempts, we developed a working prototype using the Joule Thief circuit - an elegant solution that could power LEDs using nearly depleted batteries that would otherwise be discarded.

Efficient Power Usage

Works with voltages down to 0.4V, utilizing residual energy from 70% down to 13% battery capacity

Optimal Brightness

Achieves maximum LED brightness even with nearly depleted batteries

Sustainable Design

Uses recycled materials where possible, partnering with battery recycling programs

Technical Specifications

Circuit Components

  • 1kÎ© resistor
  • 2N3904 transistor
  • LTL-307EE LED
  • 10uH permeability ferrite core

Performance Metrics

  • Minimum working voltage: 0.4V
  • Operating time: 72 hours on two depleted AA batteries
  • Energy efficiency: Uses battery from 70% to 13% capacity

Impact & Future

The project demonstrated the importance of persistence in design, taking 13 attempts to achieve success. It also highlighted how technical solutions can address significant social challenges, while emphasizing the value of user feedback in the design process.

Future Developments

  • Increasing light output for larger spaces while maintaining efficiency
  • Improving light diffusion for better coverage
  • Scaling production using sustainable materials
